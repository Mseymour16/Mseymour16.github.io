Project 1

Why We Care:

We can see from the data collected, which measures followers, total likes, average likes, posts, and an "influence score", that as your followers, posts, and likes increase, your influence increases as well.

All of these elements listed have a direct linear relationship with the influence score. As your followers, posts, and likes increase, your influence on others increases. This can matter a lot in terms of marketing; for example, it can make companies lots of money through advertising.

We are trying to answer these following questions with the given data:

-Is there a positive, statistically significant correlation between the number of followers an influencer has and their influence score?

-Is there a positive, statistically significant correlation between the average likes an influencer has on a post and their influence score?

-Is there a positive, statistically significant correlation between the total number of likes an influencer has and their influence score?

-Is there a positive, statistically significant correlation between the number of posts an influencer has made and their influence score?

Approach to Answering:

I answered these questions by looking at specific columns of data within the dataset, cleaning them, and visualizing them. I converted the units K, M, and B to their respective conversions. I got rid of all columns except the influence score, number of followers, average likes, number of posts, and the total number of likes.

Our Findings:

With the data I collected, I found that when comparing the number of followers, average number of likes, total likes, and number of posts, to a celebrity's influence score.
Although all the comparisons cause a direct linear incline, the number of followers that a celebrity has causes the largest increase on their influence score.

Interpretation of Findings and Threats to Validity:

From these findings, we can see that the answers to all of our questions is yes. None of these findings show a descrease in influence score; All of them show a direct linear increase in X to influence score. A threat to validity of this could be that it is not updated daily to include the growing numbers of likes, posts, and followers that a celebrity might have.
